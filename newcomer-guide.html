Newcomer’s Guide to Healthcare, Privacy, and Jobs in Canada

The Problem with Language Barriers

People who don’t speak English or French well may get worse healthcare. When doctors and patients can’t understand each other, it can lead to mistakes. For example, a patient’s private health details might be shared with the wrong person, or a patient might agree to a treatment they don’t fully understand.

1. Healthcare: Why Language Matters

What’s Important:
  • Doctors may misunderstand your symptoms or give incorrect treatment.
  • You might sign forms without understanding what they mean.
What to Do:
  • Ask for a professional interpreter at hospitals or clinics (it’s your right!).
  • Learn key health words in English/French (e.g., “pain,” “allergy”).
  • Use free language classes like LINC/CLIC (see resources below).

2. Privacy Policies: Don’t Get Tricked

What’s Important:
  • Privacy rules are often in English or French and can be confusing.
  • You might share private info without meaning to.
What to Do:
  • Ask someone you trust to translate privacy forms.
  • Before signing, check:
    • What data is collected? (e.g., name, birthdate)
    • Who gets your data? (e.g., banks, advertisers)
    • What are the risks? (e.g., identity theft)

3. Jobs: Why Language Skills Are Key

What’s Important:
  • Most jobs require English or French.
  • Without language skills, you may miss out even if you're highly qualified.
What to Do:
  • Take free LINC (English) or CLIC (French) classes.
  • Practice job-related words (e.g., “customer service”).
  • Join workshops at newcomer centers to improve résumé/interview skills.

4. Free Language Classes: Your Path to Success

What’s Important:
  • Understanding English/French helps with jobs, doctors, and digital safety.
What to Do:
  • If you're a permanent resident or protected person, you qualify for free classes.
  • Search for LINC/CLIC courses near you on the Canada.ca site.
  • Contact local orgs like YMCA for enrollment support.
