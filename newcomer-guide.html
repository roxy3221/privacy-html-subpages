Newcomer’s Guide to Healthcare, Privacy & Jobs

If English or French isn't your first language, navigating services like healthcare or job applications can be confusing. Here's how to stay safe and informed.

1. Healthcare: Why Language Matters

Problem: Doctors may misinterpret symptoms. You might sign consent forms you don’t understand.

  • Ask for a professional interpreter at clinics (it's your right).
  • Learn key health words: "allergy", "pain", etc.
  • Use free government language classes (LINC/CLIC).
2. Privacy Policies: Don’t Get Tricked

Problem: Privacy forms are often in English/French. You might share personal info without realizing it.

  • Ask someone you trust to help read forms.
  • Check what data is collected, who gets it, and how long it’s kept.
3. Jobs: Why Language Skills Matter

Fact: Most Canadian jobs need English or French, even if you're highly skilled.

  • Take free LINC/CLIC classes for language training.
  • Learn job-specific words: “customer service”, “safety training”.
  • Join newcomer workshops for résumé and interview prep.
4. Free Language Classes

Learning a language helps with health, jobs, and avoiding scams.

  • Free for permanent residents and protected persons.
  • Find LINC/CLIC classes
  • YMCA Newcomer Services
