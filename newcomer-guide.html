A Newcomer’s Guide to Healthcare, Privacy, and Jobs in Canada

The Problem with Language Barriers
People who don’t speak English or French well may get worse healthcare. When doctors and patients can’t understand each other, it can lead to mistakes. For example, a patient’s private health details might be shared with the wrong person, or a patient might agree to a treatment they don’t fully understand.

1. Healthcare: Why Language Matters

What’s Important: If you don’t speak English or French well, it can be hard to get good healthcare. Doctors and nurses might misunderstand your symptoms, or you might sign forms without knowing what they mean.

What to Do:

  • Ask for a professional interpreter at hospitals or clinics (it’s your right!).
  • Learn key health words in English/French (e.g., “pain,” “allergy”).
  • Use free language classes like LINC/CLIC (see resources below).
2. Privacy Policies: Don’t Get Tricked

What’s Important: Privacy policies are often in English or French. If you sign them without understanding, you might unknowingly share your address, phone number, or financial info with strangers.

What to Do:

  • Ask someone you trust to translate privacy forms.
  • Look for these details:
    • What data is collected?
    • Who will receive your data?
    • What are the risks (e.g., identity theft)?
3. Jobs: Why Language Skills Are Key

What’s Important: Most jobs in Canada require English or French. Without these skills, you might miss good job opportunities.

What to Do:

  • Take free government language classes (LINC for English, CLIC for French).
  • Learn job-specific vocabulary (e.g., “safety training”).
  • Join newcomer workshops to improve your résumé and interviews.
4. Free Language Classes: Your Path to Success

What’s Important: Learning English or French helps you get better healthcare, avoid scams, and find better jobs.

What to Do:

  • If you’re a permanent resident or protected person, you qualify for free classes.
  • Search for LINC/CLIC courses near you on Canada.ca.
  • Contact local settlement organizations like the YMCA for support.
