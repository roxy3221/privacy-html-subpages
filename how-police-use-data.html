How Police Use Your Personal Data in Canada

Why This Matters to You

In Canada, police and government agencies collect personal data for public safety. As a newcomer, it's important to understand what is collected, how it’s used, and how to protect your privacy.

How Your Data Is Collected

  • Biometrics: Fingerprints and photos are collected during immigration and may be stored for 15+ years or until age 100.
  • Social Media: Police can access public content like friends lists, tags, and locations—even if your profile is set to private.

Privacy Risks

  1. Changing Settings: Social platforms may change privacy rules without notice.
  2. Public ≠ Free Use: Even public posts may still be protected by privacy expectations in Canadian law.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Biometrics: Ask how and why your data will be used.
  • Social Media: Hide friends lists, turn off location tags, untag photos, and avoid sharing sensitive info.
  • Learn: Understand PIBs—government databases tracking personal data use.

Key Takeaway

Your data can be used in ways you don’t expect. Knowing your rights helps you stay safer and more informed in Canada.
