How Police Use Your Personal Data in Canada

Why This Matters to You

In Canada, police and government agencies collect personal data to prevent crime and manage public safety. However, this can affect your privacy, especially if you’re a newcomer unfamiliar with Canadian laws. Below is what you need to know to protect yourself.

How Your Data Is Collected and Used

1. Biometrics (Fingerprints and Photos)

  • Collected during immigration, refugee, or visa applications
  • Stored by RCMP and IRCC
  • Kept for:
    • Minimum 15 years
    • Until age 100 if you become a permanent resident, claim refugee status, or face deportation

2. Social Media Monitoring (SOCMINT)

  • Police may review public posts (even if your profile is “private”)
  • Examples of exposed data:
    • Friends lists on Facebook
    • Location tags on X/Twitter
    • Photos you’re tagged in by others

Privacy Risks You Should Know

  1. Social Media Settings Are Tricky: Privacy rules change often, and platforms push public sharing.
  2. “Public” Posts Aren’t Always Fair Game: Canadian law may still recognize an expectation of privacy.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Biometrics: Ask why your data is needed and how it will be used.
  • Social Media:
    • Lock profiles: Hide friends lists and turn off location tags.
    • Review tagged content and untag yourself.
    • Avoid posting sensitive details like job plans or travel dates.
  • Stay Informed: Learn about PIBs — government databases that track data collection and sharing.

Key Takeaway

Your data — including biometrics and social media activity — may be used in ways you didn’t expect. Knowing your rights and securing your information helps you live more safely and confidently in Canada.
