How Police Use Your Personal Data in Canada

Why This Matters to You

In Canada, police and government agencies collect personal data to prevent crime and manage public safety. However, this can affect your privacy, especially if you’re a newcomer unfamiliar with Canadian laws. Below is what you need to know to protect yourself.

How Your Data Is Collected and Used

1. Biometrics (Fingerprints and Photos)

  • Collected when you apply for immigration, refugee status, or a visa.
  • Stored by the RCMP and IRCC.
  • Retention: At least 15 years, or until your 100th birthday if:
    • You become a permanent resident
    • You claim refugee status
    • You are deemed inadmissible
    • You have a deportation order

2. Social Media Monitoring (SOCMINT)

  • Police may review public posts to investigate crimes.
  • Your data may be visible even if your profile is private:
    • Friends lists on Facebook
    • Location tags on Twitter/X
    • Photos or info shared by others
Privacy Risks You Should Know
  1. Tricky Privacy Settings: Platforms change rules, encouraging public sharing.
  2. Public ≠ Free Use: Even public posts can be protected under Canadian privacy laws.
How to Protect Yourself

Biometrics

  • Ask why data is needed and how it will be used.

Social Media

  • Lock profiles: hide friends lists and turn off location tags.
  • Remove tags from posts you didn’t create.
  • Don’t post sensitive personal details (job plans, travel dates).

Stay Informed

  • Learn about PIBs: databases that track how your data is stored/shared.
Key Takeaway

Your data—like biometrics or social media activity—can be used in ways you don’t expect. By knowing your rights and taking simple steps, you can protect your privacy in Canada.
