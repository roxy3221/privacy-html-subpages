Protecting Yourself from Scams

Why This Matters

Newcomers to Canada are often targeted by scammers because they may be unfamiliar with Canadian systems, laws, or common scams. A survey found that 7 out of 10 newcomers feel more at risk of financial scams than other Canadians. Learning how to spot scams and protect your personal information is critical to avoiding losses and stress.

Common Scams Targeting Newcomers

  • Fake Job Postings: Require fees or personal info like your SIN.
  • Phishing Attempts: Messages pretending to be from banks or IRCC (e.g., “Your SIN is suspended!”).
  • Impersonating Officials: Fake CRA or police calls demanding payment or personal details.
  • Immigration Scams: Promises of faster PR or jobs for money.
  • Social Media Scams: Fake accounts selling goods or offering romance to steal info or money.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Limit Exposure:
    • Carry only essential ID (driver’s license, health card).
    • Shred documents with personal info.
    • Avoid sharing sensitive info on social media.
  • Enhance Security:
    • Use strong passwords (e.g., Toronto2024!).
    • Lock your mailbox.
    • Keep your devices updated.
  • Stay Vigilant:
    • Review your bank statements.
    • Get a free credit report from Equifax or TransUnion.
    • Delete suspicious messages.
  • Ask the 5 Ws Before Sharing Info:
    • Who wants my data?
    • Why do they need it?
    • What will it be used for?
    • Where will it be stored?
    • When will it be deleted?

How to Report Scams

  • Contact the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre (CAFC): Call 1-888-495-8501 or report online.
  • Contact your bank if money was stolen.
  • Report immigration scams to IRCC.
