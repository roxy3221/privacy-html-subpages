Protecting Yourself from Scams

Why This Matters

Newcomers to Canada are often targeted by scammers because they may be unfamiliar with Canadian systems, laws, or common scams. A survey found that 7 out of 10 newcomers feel more at risk of financial scams than other Canadians. Learning how to spot scams and protect your personal information is critical to avoiding losses and stress.

Common Scams Targeting Newcomers

  • Fake Job Postings: Require fees or personal info like your SIN.
  • Phishing Attempts: Messages pretending to be from banks or IRCC.
  • Impersonating Officials: Fake CRA or police calls demanding payment.
  • Immigration Scams: Promises of faster PR or jobs for money.
  • Social Media Scams: Fake accounts stealing info or money.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Limit Exposure:
    • Carry only essential ID.
    • Shred documents with personal info.
    • Be cautious with sensitive info online.
  • Enhance Security:
    • Use strong passwords.
    • Lock your mailbox.
    • Keep your devices updated.
  • Stay Vigilant:
    • Check your bank statements.
    • Request free credit reports.
    • Delete suspicious messages.
  • Ask the 5 Ws Before Sharing Info:
    • Who wants my data?
    • Why do they need it?
    • What will it be used for?
    • Where will it be stored?
    • When will it be deleted?

How to Report Scams

  • Contact Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre (CAFC): 1-888-495-8501 or report online.
  • Report to your bank and immigration office (IRCC).
